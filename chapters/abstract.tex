\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

DePaso is a collaborative urban logistics digital platform for the Buenos Aires Metropolitan Area (AMBA), connecting senders ---SMEs, businesses, and individuals--- with carriers willing to deliver packages along routes they already travel regularly. The project introduces a hybrid model that combines dedicated transport with collaborative transport, leveraging idle vehicle capacity to reduce costs, CO\textsubscript{2} emissions, and delivery times.

The platform comprises four main technical components: (1)~a computer vision load classifier that determines package type and size from a photograph; (2)~a multivariable assignment algorithm that weights geospatial compatibility, route deviation, load type, carrier reputation, and time window; (3)~real-time shipment tracking; and (4)~a CO\textsubscript{2} savings calculator benchmarked against dedicated transport, using IPCC emission factors.

This document corresponds to the 25\% milestone of the Final Engineering Project and presents the theoretical framework underpinning the design decisions, the state of the art mapping academic research and existing solutions, and the results of the first user-research cycle: a survey with 145 responses from AMBA residents and workers. Quantitative findings show high willingness to use collaborative shipping (71\%), a strong preference for price transparency (87\% cite cost as the primary criterion), and an actionable pricing gap between senders' willingness to pay (ARS~3,000--6,000 for a small package) and carriers' minimum acceptable compensation (ARS~2,500--5,000), validating the economic feasibility of the proposed model.

\textbf{Keywords:} collaborative logistics, crowdsourced delivery, last-mile logistics, two-sided markets, computer vision, AMBA.
